Sri Lanka's Turtle Population

Sri Lanka is known for its beautiful beaches and rich marine biodiversity, including several species of sea turtles. Sri Lanka is home to five species of sea turtles. Unfortunately, a number of human activities, such as poaching, egg gathering, fishing, and coastal development, pose a threat to Sri Lanka's turtle species. In response, Sri Lanka has implemented a number of conservation measures, including the creation of marine reserves, harsher fishing rules, and community education initiatives. Despite these efforts, Sri Lanka's turtle population remains vulnerable and continued conservation efforts are essential to ensure the survival of these magnificent creatures.

Hikkaduwa Turtle Hatchery

A few kilometers north of the main Hikkaduwa town is where you'll find the Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Center, a facility dedicated to saving some of the world's most threatened turtle species. On the island's southern coast, there are a great number of turtle hatcheries. It began as a community initiative to help safeguard the southern coast's biodiversity, but it has now grown into a full-fledged hatchery and rescue facility with devoted crews that are on duty 24 hours a day to study, look after, and safeguard various turtle species.

Other Turtle Hatcheries

Visitors can visit and donate to a number of turtle hatcheries in Sri Lanka, including the Kosgoda Turtle Conservation Project and the Bentota Turtle Hatchery. These hatcheries gather turtle eggs from Sri Lankan beaches and safeguard them from scavengers and interference from people until they hatch.
