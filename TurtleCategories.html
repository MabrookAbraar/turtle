Turtle Diversity In Sri Lanka

Turtle Types and Species



Green Turtle


The green sea turtle holds the distinction of being the largest hard-shelled sea turtle species. What sets them apart from their counterparts is their predominantly herbivorous diet, consisting mainly of seagrasses and algae. Interestingly, their name does not stem from the color of their shells, but rather from the greenish hue exhibited by their fat.

Hawksbill Turtle


The Hawksbill Turtle is rarer than the Green Turtle and highly endangered. It is smaller, reaching a maximum length of 90 cm and weighing 50-70 kg. Its narrow head and bird-like beak are used to catch prey in small cracks, giving it its name. Despite its size, it has a beautiful shell with 13 symmetrical, vibrant sections.

Loggerhead Turtles


The Loggerhead Turtle is easily recognized by its distinctively large head and is typically colored red and brown. These are predominantly carnivores that may reach a maximum height of 1 m and weigh between 170 and 200 kg. Their big, powerful jaws are perfect for crushing mollusks and crustaceans, which are their favorite food.

Leatherback Turtle


The Leatherback Turtle, the largest of Sri Lanka's five kinds and a critically endangered Giant, is tragically close to being extinct. With its black and white striped shell and lengthy front flippers, this one is easy to identify. The carapace, which gives an appearance of leather, is actually a layer of thin, durable, rubbery skin dotted with hundreds of bone plates. It is the only species of sea turtle that lacks a hard shell.

Olive Ridley Turtle


The name of this sea turtle is derived from its olive green shell, and it is the most abundant species among sea turtles. Its unique nesting behavior, selecting only a limited number of locations, makes it vulnerable to disruptions that could affect the entire population. Therefore, protecting and preserving these nesting sites is crucial for the survival of this turtle species.
