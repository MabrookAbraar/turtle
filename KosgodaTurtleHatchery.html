About

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery is a non-profit organization based in Kosgoda City, Sri Lanka. Hatchries are dedicated to protecting sea turtle eggs and raising fry in a safe environment until they are ready to be released into the ocean.

The hatchery was founded in 1981 and has since released over a million sea turtles into the ocean. The hatchery also conducts sea turtle research and educates locals and tourists about sea turtle conservation.

Visitors to the hatchery can learn about Sri Lanka's different species of sea turtles, observe newly hatched turtles in tanks, and even release baby turtles into the ocean. The hatchery also has a small museum where visitors can learn about the hatchery's history and commitment to sea turtle conservation.

In addition to conservation efforts, the hatchery also serves as a rehabilitation center for injured sea turtles. At the hatchery, injured sea turtles are treated and cared for until they have recovered enough to be released into the ocean.

Overall, Kosgoda's Sea Turtle Hatchery is a great place to learn about sea turtles and their conservation, and to support the important work that is being done to protect these amazing creatures.
