About

Bentota Turtle Hatchery is a turtle conservation project based in the town of Induruwa, near Bentota, Sri Lanka. The project began in 1988 with the aim of protecting sea turtle eggs from predators and poachers and releasing hatchlings into the ocean.

The hatchery is run by a non-profit organization called the Sea Turtle Conservation Society and relies on donations and admission fees to fund its operations. Visitors to the hatchery can learn about the different species of sea turtles that live in Sri Lanka's waters, the threats they face and the conservation efforts that are being made to protect them.

The Hatchery has several tanks that are used to incubate rescued sea turtle eggs and raise hatchlings prior to their release. Newly hatched turtles are released into the ocean at night to increase their chances of survival. Visitors can participate in this activity for an additional fee.

In addition to conservation work, hatcheries provide employment opportunities for local residents and serve as an educational resource for schools and community groups.
